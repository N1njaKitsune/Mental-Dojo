Design Intent
The Techniques section is a passive learning and review area. Students can browse techniques they've unlocked, preview upcoming ones, and study details at their own pace. Unlike the Nendō dojo (which is active training and scoring), this is reference material with no performance pressure.

Achievements are the recognition and motivation layer. They reward consistency, exploration, and mastery across disciplines. Some achievements are discipline-specific (complete 10 Metsuke challenges), others are cross-cutting (train 3 days in a row). Achievement data is visible to the Parent section and contributes to overall student Aura progression.

6. Cross-Section Data Flows

Instructor → Student Profile
Instructor grades and session feedback update student profile belt/aura data in real-time. Grading events trigger profile refresh and parent notifications.
Nendō Session Completion → Training History + Aura XP
Completed training sessions populate training history with timestamps, scores, and duration. Session scores contribute to aura progression and achievement triggers.
Achievement Triggers → Parent Notification
When a student earns an achievement, parents receive notification if that achievement is marked as "parent-visible". Notifications link to achievement details.
Student Progress Data → Parent Section
Profile aura stage, achievement list, training stats (filtered for parent view), and top achievements are synchronized to the Parent dashboard for monitoring and encouragement.
Integration Documentation
Detailed cross-section integration specs live in _SHARED/integration/ and the main _MASTER/ARCHITECTURE.md. The Student Section receives data from Instructor grading and pushes progress visibility to the Parent section via shared data models and event hooks.

8. Non-Negotiable Rules

  • Landscape only. No portrait scroll. All screens designed for landscape orientation on phone and tablet.
  • Age-appropriate content. All designs, copy, and interactions must be appropriate for 7–9 year olds. User-tested on target age group before launch.
  • No belt language. Students progress through Aura stages, not belts or ranks. Language must be consistent with Aura terminology.
  • Device compatibility. Must work on phone landscape (375×667 min) and tablet landscape (768×1024+). Tested on iOS Safari, Chrome Android, and tablet browsers.
  • Student data must be parent-visible. Core profile, aura, achievements, and training summaries surface in the Parent section. Privacy settings control granularity.
  • Profile is the single entry point. All student features are accessed from the Student Profile. No direct navigation to other sections bypasses the profile.
  • Nendō Dojo is separate. Metsuke, Mushin, and Tachi training are managed in a separate architecture document and appear as a single entry point from the Profile.
  • Aura is unified progress. All sections contribute to and reference the same Aura progression system. No conflicting belt or level systems.
